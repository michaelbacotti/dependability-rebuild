Overnight Headlines

WTI crude +2.58% to $87.10 on continued Iran-Hormuz escalation — energy complex extends its two-week bid. WTI broke above $87 overnight, the highest level since the mid-June Iran-Israel escalation window, with the bid concentrated in the front-month futures contract (CL=F) and flowing through to USO (+3.42% pre-market to $132.87 vs Tuesday's $128.47 close). The driver is the continued reporting cycle around US-Iran tensions in the Strait of Hormuz; the contained-oil regime that defined the late-June/early-July tape is now absorbing a fresh geopolitical premium. USO closed Tuesday already +2.36% on Iran-Hormuz news, so Wednesday's overnight extension implies the market is pricing continued escalation rather than de-escalation. For long-term investors: the $85-90 WTI band is the new operating range for the contained-oil regime; a sustained break above $90 would re-introduce the inflation-fear narrative that pressured high-multiple tech in mid-June, while a fade back below $83 would confirm de-escalation. XLE pre-market is +0.99% to $59.08 (vs Tuesday's $58.50 close), consistent with the upstream-energy bid extending through the broader energy complex.

TSMC and ASML Q2 prints are today's defining catalysts — the structural reads on AI capex durability. TSMC reported Q2 results overnight (Taiwan-listed TSM, US ADR cross-listed), with consensus looking for another capex-guide confirmation after the company's July 15 raise of 2026 capex guidance from $52-56 billion to $60-64 billion. The structural question for today's print: does TSMC's 2026 capex guide hold, raise further, or trim? ASML's bookings print (lithography system orders) is the cross-validating read for the broader semiconductor equipment cycle — consensus expectations are for another record bookings print that confirms EUV demand breadth across TSMC, Samsung, and Intel. The market is pricing continued AI capex strength into the prints (XLK pre-market is +0.10% to $180.78, essentially flat as traders await the binary catalyst). For long-term investors: TSMC's capex guidance is the leading indicator for hyperscaler AI demand (Microsoft, Google, Meta, Amazon — all reporting Thursday-Friday); ASML's bookings confirm the equipment cycle has multi-customer breadth. The print is the structural validation or refutation of the Q3 setup; a positive print extends the multi-year AI infrastructure thesis; a negative surprise would compress multiples across the AI-infrastructure cohort.

10-year Treasury yield drifts +3 bps to 4.63% — reflation trade vs. rate-sensitive sectors. The 10Y pushed from Tuesday's 4.60% close to 4.63% overnight, the highest reading since May 2024. The driver is a combination of firmer UK inflation data (UK June CPI +2.6% YoY vs +2.8% expected, per Yahoo Finance economic calendar) and continued commodity-driven inflation pressure. The bond market is not pricing an accelerated Fed cut path — the curve is steepening modestly rather than bull-flattening, consistent with the "growthNominal" thesis. For long-term investors: a 4.63% 10Y is a headwind for long-duration assets (TLT pre-market flat at $83.69, reflecting the modest yield drift; XLRE and XLU modestly bid) but is consistent with the steeper-curve thesis that supports financials (XLF) and energy (XLE). Watch whether the 10Y drift is orderly (driven by data flow) or abrupt (driven by a positioning unwind); an orderly drift to 4.65% is manageable, an abrupt move above 4.70% would be a warning signal for equity multiples.
