What actually happened by the close

The M&A whisper around regional banks and payments names did not produce a deal in the cash session, but two things happened that matter. First, the 10Y yield held below 4.4% all day, finishing at 4.39% — that is meaningful because the previous week's brief spike to 4.46% had created resistance at 4.45%. Settling below 4.4% keeps the door open for a continued re-steepening of the 2s10s curve, which favors net interest margin expansion stories at the regional banks. Second, the Fed-speak calendar was light Monday but Tuesday brings two regional Fed presidents; the consensus trade is to fade any hawkish headline given the recent trend in inflation data.

Sector moves worth noting

Financials outperformed modestly with the regional bank index up 0.6% on the day. Payments lagged slightly — Visa and Mastercard were flat while PayPal underperformed on no specific catalyst. Energy reversed from the morning's bid as crude gave back the OPEC+ premium; refiners held in. Tech was mixed with semis bid on continued AI capex tailwinds (ASML reporting after Tuesday's close was a known catalyst, not a new one).

Lessons for tomorrow's tape

The key takeaway is that low-volume, no-data sessions tend to favor continuation of the prior week's theme. Last week's theme was a softening inflation narrative plus a continued bid for the regional banks. With no major data until Thursday's CPI and no earnings until ASML after Tuesday's close, expect the 10Y to remain the primary market mover. Watch 4.36% on the downside — a break below opens room to 4.30% and that would be a meaningful tailwind for the duration-sensitive names that have been struggling since the May highs.

The risk to that view is a hawkish surprise from either of Tuesday's Fed speakers. If Williams or Bowman (both scheduled) signal that the July 30 FOMC meeting is live, expect the 10Y to test 4.45% again and the regional bank trade to stall. Position sizing should reflect that we are in a low-conviction window until Thursday's CPI clears.
