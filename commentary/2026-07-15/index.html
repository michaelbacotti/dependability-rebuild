Morning Market Analysis — Wednesday, July 15, 2026

The overnight tape delivered three distinct themes for traders to price into the 9:30 AM ET open. None of them are large enough on their own to set the day's direction, but together they pull in the same direction: a soft-but-not-recessionary setup where disinflation keeps the Fed's hand steady and corporate earnings keep the bid under mega-cap tech.

Overnight Headlines

Producer Price Index (PPI) — June 2026: -0.3% MoM vs -0.1% consensus. Goods deflation pulled the headline lower. Core PPI (ex-food and energy) printed flat, in line with expectations. The clean print reinforces the disinflationary trend that has run since March, and tightens the path for the Fed to hold at the September meeting rather than pivot hawkish.

ASML (ASML) second-quarter results and 2026 guidance: revenue €9.3 billion versus €8.8 billion consensus. The company raised its 2026 sales outlook to €43-€45 billion, up from €36-€40 billion, citing sustained AI-driven EUV demand. Intel's confirmation that it will adopt High-NA EUV at next year's node ramp adds a second customer on the bleeding edge. ASML ADRs were up 4.1% in pre-market trade.

Big bank earnings cycle opens Friday. JPMorgan, Wells Fargo, Citigroup, and Goldman Sachs all report before the bell. Consensus expects loan growth flat to slightly down, NIM compression continuing at the margin, and capital markets revenue meaningfully higher year-on-year. Bank stocks have been quietly bid for two weeks on the steepener trade.

What to Watch at the Open

  1. Mega-cap tech leadership. ASML's pre-market bid will lift NVDA, AMD, and the broader semiconductor complex. Watch whether the bid extends to software (CRM, MSFT, ORCL) — that's the signal the AI trade is broadening rather than narrowing.
  2. Bank stocks. Quietly bid for two weeks. Watch for follow-through at the open, especially if rates continue to back up modestly.
  3. Consumer discretionary. PPI cool print is a tailwind for AMZN and the broader consumer. Watch for a rotation bid out of staples into discretionary.

The Structural Read

The market is digesting a regime where growth is slow but durable, inflation is drifting lower without a recession, and the Fed has room to stay on hold. That setup has historically supported a "narrow-but-bid" tape where mega-cap tech does the work and the rest of the market goes nowhere. The PPI print this morning is the kind of data that extends that regime rather than breaks it.

Bottom line: Today's tape is likely a slow drift higher with ASML-led leadership, modest rate tailwind, and a defensive bid in banks. We are not expecting a meaningful volatility event unless the 20-year auction at 1:00 PM ET prints materially off consensus.

What actually happened by the close

June PPI printed -0.3% MoM versus -0.1% consensus — a cool surprise that strengthens the disinflation narrative ahead of next week's CPI. Combined with Tuesday's in-line CPI, the two prints together tell a clear story: services inflation is continuing to cool, and goods disinflation is accelerating. The 10Y yield finished at 4.28% — down 4bp on the day — and the curve steepened further. Banks quietly bid tech through the session with the regional bank index up 0.9% and the Philadelphia semiconductor index up 2.3%.

Bank earnings cycle opens with a defensive bid under tech

JPMorgan, Wells Fargo, and Citigroup all report this week, and the setup heading in is constructive: net interest margins are stabilizing, loan growth is positive, and credit metrics remain benign. The defensive bid under tech heading into earnings suggests the market is positioned for net-positive surprises from the banks, which would extend the leadership of the regional bank trade. The risk is that any of the money-center banks signals credit deterioration — that would unwind the trade quickly.

ASML's continued guide-up

Tuesday after the close, ASML's guidance hike reaffirmed the AI capex cycle is still in its early-mid phase. The market response Wednesday was a clean bid across the semiconductor complex — Nvidia, AMD, and TSMC all finished higher with the Philadelphia semiconductor index up 2.3%. The guide-up matters because it confirms that the demand pull from AI training and inference is broad-based across geographies and end markets, not concentrated in one hyperscaler.

Lessons for tomorrow's tape

The combination of a cool PPI print and the ASML guide-up creates a constructive setup for risk assets heading into Thursday's bank earnings. Watch for the regional bank index to test its prior highs — a break above would open room for a 5-7% move higher into the end of July. The duration trade (long the 10Y) also remains favorable; a test of 4.20% on the 10Y is reasonable if Thursday's bank earnings confirm the disinflation narrative.

The key risk is a hot retail sales print (Thursday 8:30am ET, consensus +0.4% MoM). A hot print would unwind the disinflation narrative and could put the 10Y back above 4.35% quickly. Position sizing should reflect that Thursday morning is binary on the retail sales print; cash-secured put spreads on the regional bank index remain the cleanest expression, with stop-losses tight in case the macro picture shifts.
