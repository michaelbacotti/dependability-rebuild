Morning Update: TSMC and ASML Q2 Prints Set the AI-Capex Bar Today; WTI Extends Iran-Hormuz Bid to $87.10; 10Y Drifts to 4.63%

Pre-market Wed: S&P futures +0.28% to 7,530 ahead of TSMC/ASML Q2 prints. WTI +2.58% to $87.10 on Iran-Hormuz. Existing Home Sales 10 AM ET.

Morning Market Analysis — Wednesday, July 22, 2026

The pre-market tape is quietly bifurcated: equity futures are essentially flat-to-marginally-higher (ES +0.28% to 7,530.50 vs Tuesday's 7,509.20 cash close; NQ -0.54% to 29,157.50; RTY -0.24% to 2,989.70), but the underlying cross-asset tape is more telling — WTI crude +2.58% to $87.10 on continued Iran-Hormuz escalation, the 10-year Treasury yield drifting +3 basis points to 4.63% (the highest since May 2024), and gold up +1.27% to $4,122/oz. The day's defining event is the TSMC and ASML Q2 prints — the structural reads on AI capex durability that yesterday's afternoon forecast specifically flagged as "the most consequential single data point of the week." A capex guide higher or a record bookings print extends the AI infrastructure super-cycle thesis and supports XLK, NVDA, and the broader semiconductor equipment complex; a guide-down would re-test the 7,440 area and force a re-pricing of the multi-year AI capex cycle.

Overnight Headlines

WTI crude +2.58% to $87.10 on continued Iran-Hormuz escalation — energy complex extends its two-week bid. WTI broke above $87 overnight, the highest level since the mid-June Iran-Israel escalation window, with the bid concentrated in the front-month futures contract (CL=F) and flowing through to USO (+3.42% pre-market to $132.87 vs Tuesday's $128.47 close). The driver is the continued reporting cycle around US-Iran tensions in the Strait of Hormuz; the contained-oil regime that defined the late-June/early-July tape is now absorbing a fresh geopolitical premium. USO closed Tuesday already +2.36% on Iran-Hormuz news, so Wednesday's overnight extension implies the market is pricing continued escalation rather than de-escalation. For long-term investors: the $85-90 WTI band is the new operating range for the contained-oil regime; a sustained break above $90 would re-introduce the inflation-fear narrative that pressured high-multiple tech in mid-June, while a fade back below $83 would confirm de-escalation. XLE pre-market is +0.99% to $59.08 (vs Tuesday's $58.50 close), consistent with the upstream-energy bid extending through the broader energy complex.

TSMC and ASML Q2 prints are today's defining catalysts — the structural reads on AI capex durability. TSMC reported Q2 results overnight (Taiwan-listed TSM, US ADR cross-listed), with consensus looking for another capex-guide confirmation after the company's July 15 raise of 2026 capex guidance from $52-56 billion to $60-64 billion. The structural question for today's print: does TSMC's 2026 capex guide hold, raise further, or trim? ASML's bookings print (lithography system orders) is the cross-validating read for the broader semiconductor equipment cycle — consensus expectations are for another record bookings print that confirms EUV demand breadth across TSMC, Samsung, and Intel. The market is pricing continued AI capex strength into the prints (XLK pre-market is +0.10% to $180.78, essentially flat as traders await the binary catalyst). For long-term investors: TSMC's capex guidance is the leading indicator for hyperscaler AI demand (Microsoft, Google, Meta, Amazon — all reporting Thursday-Friday); ASML's bookings confirm the equipment cycle has multi-customer breadth. The print is the structural validation or refutation of the Q3 setup; a positive print extends the multi-year AI infrastructure thesis; a negative surprise would compress multiples across the AI-infrastructure cohort.

10-year Treasury yield drifts +3 bps to 4.63% — reflation trade vs. rate-sensitive sectors. The 10Y pushed from Tuesday's 4.60% close to 4.63% overnight, the highest reading since May 2024. The driver is a combination of firmer UK inflation data (UK June CPI +2.6% YoY vs +2.8% expected, per Yahoo Finance economic calendar) and continued commodity-driven inflation pressure. The bond market is not pricing an accelerated Fed cut path — the curve is steepening modestly rather than bull-flattening, consistent with the "growthNominal" thesis. For long-term investors: a 4.63% 10Y is a headwind for long-duration assets (TLT pre-market flat at $83.69, reflecting the modest yield drift; XLRE and XLU modestly bid) but is consistent with the steeper-curve thesis that supports financials (XLF) and energy (XLE). Watch whether the 10Y drift is orderly (driven by data flow) or abrupt (driven by a positioning unwind); an orderly drift to 4.65% is manageable, an abrupt move above 4.70% would be a warning signal for equity multiples.

XLE pre-market is +0.99% (consistent with the bid); energy is the only sector with broad-based pre-market strength. A 4.63% 10Y supports financials (XLF) and energy (XLE) on the steeper-curve thesis; pressures long-duration assets (TLT flat at $83.69, XLRE and XLU modestly bid). Watch the 2Y/10Y spread for confirmation of steepening vs. flattening — a steeper curve is consistent with the structural overweight on XLF and XLE.

The Structural Read

The day's defining question is whether the AI capex super-cycle has durable breadth. Yesterday's afternoon forecast specifically flagged TSMC and ASML as "the most consequential single data point of the week" — the prints are the binary validation (or refutation) of the AI infrastructure thesis that has supported the S&P 500's path from 7,500 to 7,800 over the past month. TSMC's July 15 raise of 2026 capex guidance from $52-56B to $60-64B was the first signal; ASML's record Q2 bookings is the cross-validating print. The market is positioning for a continued AI capex confirmation (XLK pre-market flat-to-positive; USO bid; gold bid as the hedge against a negative surprise); a positive print extends the structural thesis; a negative surprise would force a re-pricing of the multi-year AI infrastructure capex cycle. For long-term investors: use any pre-print volatility as the entry window for high-quality AI infrastructure names; the structural thesis is the multi-year capex cycle, not the single-day print reaction.

The cross-asset tape is signaling elevated geopolitical risk, not a regime break. WTI +2.58% to $87.10 and gold +1.27% to $4,122/oz are moves consistent with geopolitical-risk pricing (Iran-Hormuz) and inflation-pressure absorption. The 10Y at 4.63% (+3 bps overnight) reflects the same: reflation trade, not panic. Equity futures are essentially flat (ES +0.28% gap vs cash close, NQ -0.54% from Tuesday settlement, RTY -0.24%). The configuration — flat equities, bid oil and gold, modestly higher yields — is the textbook setup for a tape that is digesting the structural question (AI capex) while absorbing a fresh geopolitical premium (Iran-Hormuz). Neither the equity market nor the bond market is signaling a regime break; both are positioning for today's catalysts.

The macro backdrop remains the most constructive since the 2023 year-end rally. Disinflation is confirmed (June CPI -0.4% MoM, June PPI -0.3% MoM, June retail sales +0.2% MoM, July UoM preliminary consumer sentiment soft). The Q2 bank earnings cycle validated the financial-sector thesis (GS, JPM, BAC, MS, BLK all reported strong results in mid-July). The Q2 megacap tech earnings cycle — TSMC/ASML today, MSFT/GOOGL/META/AMZN Thursday-Friday — is the next test. The July 28-29 FOMC is 6 days away; the market is currently pricing approximately 60% probability of a September rate cut. The structural overweight on AI infrastructure, financials, US domestic growth, and US small caps (steepener thesis) remains intact pending today's TSMC/ASML prints. For long-term investors: the path of least resistance over the next 2-4 weeks is to consolidate in the 7,500-7,600 range, with the 7,500-7,550 band as the test of structural strength. A break above 7,600 on positive TSMC/ASML prints + July 28-29 FOMC dovishness would re-activate the year-end 7,800 base case. A break below 7,500 on a TSMC/ASML guide-trim would re-test the 7,400-7,450 support band.

Bottom line: Wednesday is a binary-catalyst day — TSMC + ASML Q2 prints set the structural AI capex bar for the next quarter. Pre-market is bifurcated (flat equities, bid oil/gold, modestly higher yields), consistent with positioning for a positive print while hedging the binary risk. The first 30 minutes of cash market trading will confirm whether the structural uptrend re-asserts or extends the consolidation. Use the existing 7,500 support test as the entry window for high-quality AI infrastructure names ahead of the next catalyst cluster (MSFT/GOOGL/META/AMZN Thursday-Friday, FOMC July 28-29).
