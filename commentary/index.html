Morning Update: PPI Cool Surprise, ASML Guides Up Again, Banks Quietly Bid Tech

Pre-market Tuesday: Producer Price Index printed -0.3% MoM versus -0.1% consensus — a cool surprise that strengthens the disinflation narrative ahead of next week's CPI. ASML's second-half guidance hike reaffirms AI capex cycle. Bank earnings cycle opens this week with a defensive bid under tech into the open.

The overnight tape delivered three distinct themes for traders to price into the 9:30 AM ET open. None of them are large enough on their own to set the day's direction, but together they pull in the same direction: **a soft-but-not-recessionary setup where disinflation keeps the Fed's hand steady and corporate earnings keep the bid under mega-cap tech**.

**Producer Price Index (PPI) — June 2026: -0.3% MoM vs -0.1% consensus.** Goods deflation pulled the headline lower. Core PPI (ex-food and energy) printed flat, in line with expectations. The clean print reinforces the disinflationary trend that has run since March, and tightens the path for the Fed to hold at the September meeting rather than pivot hawkish.

**ASML (ASML) second-quarter results and 2026 guidance:** revenue €9.3 billion versus €8.8 billion consensus. The company raised its 2026 sales outlook to €43-€45 billion, up from €36-€40 billion, citing sustained AI-driven EUV demand. Intel's confirmation that it will adopt High-NA EUV at next year's node ramp adds a second customer on the bleeding edge. ASML ADRs were up 4.1% in pre-market trade.

MORNING MARKET ANALYSIS — July 14, 2026
← Previous: July 13, 2026

Morning Update: Markets Drift into CPI Day, ASML's Earnings Setup

Pre-market Tuesday: Markets positioned for this morning's June CPI print — consensus +0.3% MoM headline, +0.2% core. ASML reports after the close today; consensus revenue €8.8B with focus on 2026 sales guide.

A quiet tape overnight, with markets coiled ahead of this morning's June CPI release and ASML's quarterly report after the close. Both events are high-impact for the broader tape: CPI sets the inflation trajectory for the Fed's September meeting, and ASML is the cleanest read on AI capex demand.

**June CPI consensus +0.3% MoM headline, +0.2% core.** Cleveland Fed Nowcast tracks the headline at +0.31%, core at +0.23%. A print in line or below would reinforce the disinflationary trend; a hot surprise would force a re-pricing of the September Fed path. Treasury futures imply a 72% probability of a 25-bp cut at the September meeting as of yesterday's close.

**ASML (ASML) earnings after the close.** Consensus revenue €8.8B, gross margin 51.2%, EPS €5.86. The market is focused on the 2026 sales guidance — currently €36-€40B — and whether China export controls have shifted the backlog meaningfully. NVDA, AMD, and the broader semiconductor complex trade in sympathy with the print.

MORNING MARKET ANALYSIS — July 13, 2026

Morning Update: New Week Opens With M&A Whisper Number, 10Y Yield Holds Below 4.4%

Monday kickoff: M&A chatter in the tape (regional banks + payments names), 10Y yield steady below 4.4% into the reopen, no major economic releases today but watch the Fed-speak calendar mid-week.

A quiet start to a week loaded with bank earnings and Thursday's CPI release. Markets look for direction from the upcoming earnings calendar and a heavy Fed-speak lineup, but today itself is light on hard data.

**M&A whisper numbers pick up.** A regional bank consortium is reportedly weighing a bid for a mid-cap payments processor at a 22% premium to Friday's close — the deal isn't confirmed but the option-market chatter has lifted related call skew. Watch regional bank ETFs (KRE) and payment processors at the open.

**10-year yield holds below 4.4%.** The Treasury complex is steady into the reopen, with the 10Y at 4.36% as of the Asian close. Auction supply ramps this week ($39B 10Y Tuesday, $35B 30Y Wednesday) — demand gauges matter more than headline size.
