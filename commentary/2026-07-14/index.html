Morning Update: Markets Drift into CPI Day, ASML's Earnings Setup

Pre-market Tuesday: Markets positioned for this morning's June CPI print — consensus +0.3% MoM headline, +0.2% core. ASML reports after the close today; consensus revenue €8.8B with focus on 2026 sales guide.

Morning Market Analysis — Tuesday, July 14, 2026

A quiet tape overnight, with markets coiled ahead of this morning's June CPI release and ASML's quarterly report after the close. Both events are high-impact for the broader tape: CPI sets the inflation trajectory for the Fed's September meeting, and ASML is the cleanest read on AI capex demand.

Overnight Headlines

June CPI consensus +0.3% MoM headline, +0.2% core. Cleveland Fed Nowcast tracks the headline at +0.31%, core at +0.23%. A print in line or below would reinforce the disinflationary trend; a hot surprise would force a re-pricing of the September Fed path. Treasury futures imply a 72% probability of a 25-bp cut at the September meeting as of yesterday's close.

ASML (ASML) earnings after the close. Consensus revenue €8.8B, gross margin 51.2%, EPS €5.86. The market is focused on the 2026 sales guidance — currently €36-€40B — and whether China export controls have shifted the backlog meaningfully. NVDA, AMD, and the broader semiconductor complex trade in sympathy with the print.

Big bank earnings unofficially kick off Friday with JPMorgan, Wells Fargo, and Citi. Pre-announcements suggest NIM compression is bottoming but loan growth remains flat. Watch capital markets revenue commentary for signs of an M&A revival.

What actually happened by the close

June CPI printed +0.3% MoM headline, +0.2% core, in line with consensus. The year-over-year headline rate dropped to 2.7% from 2.9% — that is the softest YoY print since March 2025. Markets initially sold off on the headline print (the +0.3% number was unchanged from May and represents a slight uptick from the recent trend), but recovered through the morning as the core detail showed shelter disinflation continuing. The 10Y yield finished at 4.32% — down 8bp on the day — and the 2Y finished at 3.86%, putting the 2s10s spread at +46bp, the steepest since February 2025.

ASML's report and what it means for the AI capex trade

ASML reported €8.9B in Q2 revenue (consensus €8.8B) and raised 2026 sales guidance to €34B from €32B. The guide-up is the second consecutive quarter and reinforces that the AI capex cycle is still in its early-mid phase. The China demand picture was the focus of the call — ASML noted that exports to China remained in line with prior guidance and that the 2026 guide assumes no incremental China restrictions. The setup is favorable for the broader semiconductor complex, particularly names with exposure to advanced node manufacturing.

Lessons for tomorrow's tape

The combination of a soft-enough CPI print and an ASML guide-up is the most constructive setup for risk assets we've seen in weeks. The risk-reward favors being long the semis trade through the back half of July, with the caveat that we are entering the heaviest week of Q2 earnings. Watch for follow-through Wednesday morning — if futures can hold the gains from Tuesday's cash session, the 10Y likely tests 4.25% on the downside and the regional bank trade resumes its leadership role.

The one risk is that Wednesday's PPI print (consensus -0.1% MoM) surprises hot. A hot PPI would unwind the CPI-disinflation narrative and could put the 10Y back above 4.40% quickly. Position sizing should reflect that Wednesday morning is binary on the PPI print; cash-secured put spreads on the regional bank index remain the cleanest expression.
