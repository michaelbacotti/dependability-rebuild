Options Education

A structured curriculum for traders who want to understand options markets — from first principles to strategy implementation.

Options Education is the foundational layer of everything we publish at Dependability Holdings LLC. If you're new to options, start here. If you're an experienced trader who wants to tighten your mental models, you'll find something useful here too — the fundamentals never stop mattering, and the gaps in most traders' understanding tend to live in the basics they skipped over.

We built this section because most options education online is either too simplified — endless explanations of calls and puts with no connection to how professionals actually think — or too technical, drowning readers in math before they've developed the instinct for when the math matters. Our approach sits in between: we're writing for traders who want to understand the instruments well enough to use them deliberately, not just execute them.

The curriculum covers core concepts (what options are, how they behave, why they behave that way), the Greeks (Delta, Gamma, Theta, Vega — what each one measures, how they interact, and when to focus on each), and strategy foundations (when to use a debit spread versus a credit spread, how to think about undefined-risk structures, how to manage positions when vol moves against you). We also include pieces on macro-adjacent topics — gold as a store of value, how interest rates flow through to option pricing — because options traders who ignore macro context are flying half blind.

Every education piece is self-contained and written to be readable in one sitting. We don't assume prior knowledge beyond general market familiarity — if you know what a stock is and that prices move, we'll take it from there. Glossary terms are linked inline, and any piece that involves strategy includes a plain-language explanation of the risk profile, not just the pitch.

New education pieces are added as we identify gaps in the existing curriculum. All content is reviewed for accuracy before publication. Sources include Cboe options reference materials, OCC publicatons, and standard financial texts.
