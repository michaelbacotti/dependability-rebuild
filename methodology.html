Research Methodology

Transparent about how we collect, analyze, and present market research.

Data Sources

Our primary market data comes from public sources: Yahoo Finance for equity and futures prices, FRED (Federal Reserve Economic Data) for macroeconomic series, CBOE for VIX and volatility surface data, and the SEC EDGAR database for regulatory filings. Where applicable, we reference proprietary data providers noted in the relevant analysis. We do not use non-public, material information in any of our research.

Analysis Framework

Our analytical framework combines three layers:

Macro context: We begin with a top-down assessment of the interest rate environment, credit conditions, and leading economic indicators. Treasury yields, the Fed Funds rate path, and the slope of the yield curve are inputs into our volatility regime assessment.

Volatility surface analysis: We monitor the VIX level, the VIX term structure (near-term vs. longer-dated futures), and the implied vs. realized volatility relationship. These inputs inform our views on whether the market is under- or over-pricing near-term risk.

Positioning and flow: We review publicly available Commitment of Traders (COT) data, ETF flow reports, and options flow analytics where available. These are secondary inputs that help us understand institutional positioning trends.

Views and Updates

Research views represent the analyst's best assessment at the time of publication. We update views as new data arrives, but we do not edit historical articles — past articles remain as records of prior analysis. The homepage always reflects current thinking. We clearly mark the date of publication on all content.
