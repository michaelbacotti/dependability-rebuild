The Dependability Research Desk publishes market commentary on a regular cadence — typically several times per week, and more frequently during high-signal events like Federal Reserve meetings, earnings season, or unexpected macro shifts. Each piece is written for active options traders who want more than headlines: they want context, interpretation, and a framework for how to think about what's happening.

Our commentary covers the full breadth of the options market universe. We write about the S&P 500 and Nasdaq, individual large-cap names, sector rotations, futures and VIX dynamics, macro forces — rates, inflation, geopolitical risk — and the positioning behavior of institutional players. We also cover fixed income and currencies when they intersect with equity volatility, because the old walls between asset classes have become increasingly porous.

Our primary lens is volatility: what the VIX is saying, how the term structure is sloping, where IV is elevated or compressed, and when the options market is pricing a move that hasn't arrived yet. We believe the gap between what the market prices and what actually happens is where the most interesting opportunities — and risks — live.

We don't publish trade alerts or price targets. What we do is give you the analytical toolkit to form your own judgment. Each piece is designed to stand alone, but taken together they build a picture of the prevailing market regime — whether volatility is elevated, compressing, trending, or range-bound, and what that means for your positioning.

New commentary is published multiple times per week. Sources include Cboe, FRED, SEC filings, and primary macro data from BLS, BEA, and the Federal Reserve.
