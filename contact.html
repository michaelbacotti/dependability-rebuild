Dependability Holdings LLC publishes independent financial research for informational purposes only. We cover S&P 500 dynamics, VIX behavior, options strategies, and broader macroeconomic trends — written for investors and analysts who want clear-eyed analysis rather than market cheerleading or sensationalism. We welcome inquiries from readers, media professionals, and institutional partners who share an interest in rigorous, independent financial research.

We are not a registered investment advisor, and nothing on this site — or in our communications — constitutes financial advice. Our research is designed to inform and educate. Any decisions you make based on our work are your own; we encourage all readers to consult a licensed financial advisor before making investment decisions.

About Our Research Process

Every article we publish goes through a multi-stage review process. We begin with primary data sources — CBOE for VIX and options data, FRED for economic indicators, SEC filings for corporate data, and government statistical releases for macroeconomic context. That data is then analyzed through the lens of our stated methodology, which is documented in full on our Methodology page. We do not trade on our research, and we do not accept compensation from any company whose securities we discuss in our analysis.

Our forecasts represent our current best thinking given available data — they are not guarantees or predictions that will necessarily prove accurate. Markets are fundamentally uncertain, and any research that treats them as predictable should be approached with skepticism. We try to express uncertainty honestly, which is why our forecast articles include explicit confidence levels and the key assumptions underlying our scenarios.

Errors and Corrections

We make every effort to ensure accuracy in our published research, but errors do occur. If you spot a factual error in any of our articles — incorrect data, a miscalculated figure, or a source that needs updating — please let us know immediately. We review correction requests promptly and update articles when warranted. Please include the article title and specific concern in your subject line so we can route it to the right analyst.

Data and Methodology Questions

If you have questions about our data sources, calculation methodology, or the assumptions underlying a specific forecast, we want to hear from you. Good methodology questions lead to better research, and we take methodological scrutiny seriously. The best way to get a thorough answer is to reference the specific article and the specific claim you are questioning. We will do our best to explain our reasoning and point you to the underlying data sources.

Our Editorial Standards

We hold our research to the standards of general-interest financial publications: well-sourced, clearly written, and free from commercial influence. We do not accept sponsored content, paid placements, or any advertising arrangement that could influence what we write. Our only revenue from outside sources is standard display advertising, which is clearly labeled and served independently of our editorial process.

We distinguish clearly between factual reporting and editorial opinion. When we state a fact — a historical rate, a verified figure from a primary source — we aim to provide the basis for that fact. When we offer an opinion or interpretation, we try to make that clear. We believe this distinction matters more as markets become increasingly saturated with content that blurs the line between analysis and advocacy.

Why We Publish This Research

Financial research has a transparency problem. Much of what is published as "analysis" is actually marketing — designed to generate leads for products, drive traffic to platforms, or promote specific investment products. We started Dependability Holdings LLC because we believed there was room for research that simply tried to be right and clear, without a commercial agenda behind it.

We are under no illusion that our research is infallible. We will be wrong sometimes — sometimes in ways that are obvious in retrospect. We try to publish our assumptions and our confidence levels alongside our forecasts so that readers can evaluate our reasoning, not just our conclusions. If you ever read something on this site and think we have gotten something wrong, we want to know. The fastest way to improve research is to be told when it has failed.

Whether you are a reader who found a typo, an analyst who thinks our VIX model needs refinement, or a journalist working on a market story — reach out. We read every message and do our best to respond substantively. Thank you for reading, and for caring enough to engage with independent financial research.
