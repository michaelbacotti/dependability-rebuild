Daily Move: Wednesday, July 1 — Hawkish FOMC Minutes + Hot ISM Drive a Steepening-Curve Rotation

As of Wednesday, July 1, 2026 (4:00 PM ET close), the S&P 500 closed at 7,483.23, down -0.22% on the day versus Tuesday June 30's 7,499.36 all-time high close and +1.75% WTD from Friday June 26's 7,354.02 close. The session printed a modest pullback from the all-time high as the market absorbed two binary catalysts at 2 PM ET: the ISM Manufacturing PMI (June) at 53.3 (estimate 54.0, prior 54.0 May) — the third consecutive month of expansionary territory above 50 — and the FOMC minutes from the June 17-18 meeting which reaffirmed Chair Warsh's hawkish SEP hike bias. The combined catalyst drove a steepening-curve rotation: financials (XLF +2.18%) and communication services (XLC +2.43%) led the bid, while technology (XLK -2.57%, QQQ -1.53%) sold off on the discount-rate response, and oil tested new sub-$70 lows (WTI $68.09, -2.03% on the day). The pattern across today's 11 GICS sectors: rate-sensitives and curve-steepeners (XLF, XLC, XLV, XLB) outperformed discount-rate-sensitives (XLK, XLY, XLI, XLU, XLE) by a clear margin.

What Drove the Tape

Wednesday's tape was the textbook "hawkish Fed + hot data = rotation, not retreat" pattern, and the S&P's -0.22% pullback from the all-time high is structurally constructive. The dual catalyst at 2 PM ET — ISM Manufacturing at 53.3 (third consecutive month above 50) and FOMC minutes reaffirming Warsh's hike bias — initially looked like a "good news is bad news" setup (strong data = more Fed tightening = discount rate up = tech down). But the cross-asset response tells a more nuanced story: the 10Y yield rose +10 bp on the day (the largest single-day move since the Warsh SEP flip on June 18), the curve steepened (TNX +10 bp, TLT -1.04%), and financials outperformed tech by a wide margin (XLF +2.18% vs XLK -2.57%, a 4.75 percentage point sector spread — the largest single-day sector dispersion of 2026). The structural read: the equity tape is positioning for a "soft-landing, no-rate-cut" regime where growth is intact but the discount rate stays elevated, which favors financials (steeper curve, NIM expansion), energy (commodity base), and quality cyclicals over high-multiple tech. The 1-month target of 7,500 was met intraday on Tuesday (7,508.29) and is now within 0.22% of the close; the 3-month target of 7,600 is under review pending Thursday's Jobs Report and Friday's markets-closed holiday.

The FOMC minutes were hawkish-but-mechanical, not a new shock. The minutes from the June 17-18 meeting (Warsh's debut, unanimous 12-0 vote, hawkish dot plot, removal of forward guidance, five new task forces) reaffirmed that the Committee sees rate cuts as inappropriate until inflation is durably moving toward 2%. The market's reaction — 10Y +10 bp to 4.47%, TLT -1.04%, financials bid, tech sold off — was the textbook steepening-curve response. For long-term investors: this is a confirmation of the Warsh regime, not a new information event. The bond market has been pricing this since the June 18 SEP flip, and the equity tape's response today is the second-order "rotation, not retreat" recalibration. The structural overweight on financials (steeper-curve beneficiaries), energy (commodity base), and quality cyclicals (industrials, discretionary on weakness) is intact; the tactical underweight on high-multiple tech with limited AI exposure (the cohort that led Tuesday's all-time-high rally) is also intact.

The ISM Manufacturing beat is the structural bullish signal — and the market under-reacted to it. ISM Mfg at 53.3 (estimate 54.0, prior 54.0 May) is the third consecutive month above 50 and the highest sustained reading since mid-2022. The sub-components were: New Orders 56.0 (vs 56.8 prior), Production 52.2 (vs 54.3), Employment 49.7 (vs 48.6), Prices Paid 73.0 (vs 82.1 prior — the disinflation impulse continues), New Export Orders 48.5 (vs 50.6), and Inventories 42.3. The cleanest read: US manufacturing is solidly in expansion, prices are still elevated but rolling over (the disinflation impulse is intact despite the policy-rate support), and employment in manufacturing is at the inflection. The slight miss to the 54.0 estimate is noise — the trend is unambiguously expansionary. For long-term investors: this is the "soft-landing, no-recession" data flow that supports the structural bull case. The market's -0.22% on the ISM day is a discount-rate response, not a fundamentals response; the AI capex thesis, the US manufacturing reshoring theme, and the disinflation-with-growth regime are all intact.

Tech's sell-off is a discount-rate response, not a thesis break. XLK closed at $185.62 (-2.57% on the day, still +2.49% WTD) and QQQ at $725.17 (-1.53% on the day, +2.64% WTD). The discount-rate-driven rotation out of high-multiple tech into steepening-curve beneficiaries (financials, energy, quality cyclicals) is a healthy rebalancing, not an AI-thesis abandonment. Position: structural overweight on AI infrastructure (semis, custom silicon, data-center REITs, AI power); use the hawkish-Fed sell-off as a long-term buying opportunity, not an exit signal.
