Extended Hours: Tuesday, July 21 — As of 7:00 AM ET

The pre-market setup is the most constructive since the June 25 FOMC cut. S&P 500 futures (ES) are +1.09% to 7,524.75 versus Friday's 7,443.28 cash close, on the back of a binary catalyst weekend: TSMC reported Q2 revenue of $23.7 billion (+40% YoY) and raised its full-year AI chip growth forecast; ASML reported record Q2 bookings of €5.6 billion (+26% YoY) and guided Q3 revenue above consensus. The market's initial July 15 reaction to TSMC's capex raise was negative (XLK -3.2% that day), but the confirmation print from ASML — which services TSMC, Samsung, and Intel equally — is providing the cross-validation that the investment community needed. The VIX has responded with the sharpest one-day collapse since June 26: 17.60 as of Tuesday morning, down from 18.65 Monday and 18.77 Friday. The term structure has steepened positively, with VVIX at 102.82 (down from 104.87 Friday) suggesting the vol-of-vol premium is also compressing.

Asia was the strongest pre-market catalyst driver: the Nikkei 225 closed +2.1% and the Hang Seng closed +1.8% on the back of China's Third Plenum communiqué (July 18) committing to "new productive forces" and technology self-sufficiency, which the market is reading as a commitment to continued AI and semiconductor investment. European markets opened higher (DAX +0.8%, FTSE +0.4%). The dollar is modestly stronger (DXY ICE 100.94, +0.3% from Friday), which is a modest headwind for EM and commodity prices but consistent with US growth outperformance. Gold at $4,070 (+0.9% from Friday $4,034 close) is holding the $4,000 floor on geopolitical risk (Iran-Hormuz remains contested) and central bank demand. WTI at $83.17 (+0.83% from Friday $82.49 close) is pricing a moderate Iran-Hormuz risk premium without a full escalation premium.

No major US economic data is scheduled for release today. The earnings calendar is light today but builds into a pivotal week: Netflix reports Wednesday after market close (Q2 subscriber growth consensus +6.2 million), and the major US bank earnings cycle opens Thursday with JPMorgan (Q2 EPS consensus $4.19 vs $4.26 prior) and continues Friday with Bank of America, Goldman Sachs, and Wells Fargo. The FOMC meeting on July 28-29 is 7 days away; the blackout period for Fed speakers begins Friday July 25, so no more pre-meeting signals from officials until the decision.

What to Watch

  1. 10:00 AM ET — July NAHB Housing Market Index (consensus 43 vs prior 42): The housing market has been in a modest recovery as mortgage rates ease from their October 2023 peak. A print above 43 would signal continued recovery momentum and is mildly bullish for rate-sensitive sectors (XLRE, XLU) and financials (XLF). A below-consensus print would be a disappointment but not market-moving given the light data calendar.
  1. After Market Close — Netflix Q2 earnings (NFLX): Consensus estimates Q2 revenue of $10.35 billion (+15.8% YoY) and +6.2 million paid net global subscribers. The market's focus is on advertising-tier growth and the impact of password-sharing enforcement. This is not a market-moving event in the way TSMC or ASML are, but it will set the tone for communication services (XLC) into Wednesday's session. XLK and QQQ are the cleaner expressions of the AI content/delivery thesis.
  1. FOMC Blackout Clock — 7 days to July 28-29 meeting: With no Fed speakers permitted after Friday July 25, the market is in a pre-meeting positioning window. The current futures-implied probability for a July cut is ~12% (essentially no cut), with September as the first realistic meeting (futures-implied ~65%). The key question for the July 28-29 FOMC is whether the statement language shifts from "data-dependent" to "progress on inflation" framing — a rhetorical shift that would be dollar-bullish and rate-sensitive-assets-bullish simultaneously.
