Morning Commentary

June CPI landed cleanly soft at 8:30 AM ET, and the tape executed a textbook disinflation-trade rotation in the first 90 minutes of the cash session. Headline -0.4% MoM and YoY +3.5% (vs +3.8% consensus — a 30 bp undershoot) cleared the bar that Monday's pre-print de-risking had positioned for. Core CPI printed flat at 0.0% MoM with broad-based softness across motor vehicle insurance, communication, apparel, medical care, and used vehicles, while shelter provided "meaningful relief" per the BLS release. Energy CPI did most of the deflationary heavy lifting on the headline figure (-5.7% MoM, with gasoline -7.2%), but the core flatness is the real signal — shelter disinflation that began in Q1 2026 is extending through Q2.

The cross-asset tape confirmed the dovish reaction within minutes. Ten-year yields fell -2.4 bp to 4.585% as bonds led the move; equities gapped higher at the open (SPX +0.40% to 7,545.77 cash); the VIX was crushed -4.25% to 16.43 — back below the 1-year mean of ~20, signaling the binary tail risk that Monday had priced in had resolved; gold rallied +1.61% to $4,061 on the real-rate compression thesis; and the dollar deflated -0.36% to 100.92. The lone cross-current was WTI crude +1.84% to $79.58 — the Iran-Hormuz geopolitical premium remains intact even as headline CPI energy collapsed on retail gasoline prices. That's a wholesale/retail timing-divergence, not a regime break; oil and headline CPI energy should re-converge within 2-3 weeks as retail catches down.

Sector rotation reversed Monday's pre-print positioning cleanly, which validates the framing. Monday's session was a textbook de-risk: XLK -2.49% (rate-sensitive duration compressed ahead of the binary), defensives bid (XLU +0.84%, XLP +0.57%, XLV +0.33%), gold sold -2.67% on a stagflation scare, and small caps lagged (IWM -1.02%). Today's session is the mirror image: XLK +1.42% / QQQ +1.23% led the rally, defensives deflated (XLV -1.92%, XLP -1.39%), gold ran +1.36% (GLD), small caps participated modestly (IWM +0.26%). The pattern is a clean positioning unwind, NOT a fresh regime-shift higher — participation was broad without overextension, with industrials (XLI) and consumer discretionary (XLY) both near-flat. The structural uptrend is intact: the 1-month target of 7,500 flagged in yesterday's PM forecast has been cleared (current 7,545.77 is +0.61% above it), and the bear-case window (year-end 7,000) has narrowed materially. The next tactical pivot is the 7,610 resistance flagged in Monday's PM forecast; a clean break above on follow-through buying (VIX holding sub-17, 10Y holding sub-4.60%) re-activates the year-end 7,800 base case.

Three catalysts to watch through the rest of today and into tomorrow. First, JPM and GS earnings after the close — NIM, provisions, capital return. A clean earnings cycle would push XLF through $57 and broaden the rally into rate-sensitive cyclicals; a credit-loss surprise or NIM compression would drag XLF back to $54-55 and re-tighten the rally. Second, Fed Gov. Waller at 12:30 PM ET at NYABE — Waller has been a hawkish voice; any pushback against the dovish reaction would force a 10Y re-pricing and pull SPX off the highs. Third, PPI Wednesday at 8:30 AM ET (consensus +0.2% MoM / +2.8% YoY) — a soft PPI closes the dovish-inflation-pair door opened by today's CPI; a hot PPI forces the Fed to lean harder on the labor side of the mandate and complicates rate-cut sequencing.
