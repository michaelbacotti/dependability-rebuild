Morning Brief: Tuesday, July 14 — Soft CPI Confirms Disinflation; 1-Month Target Cleared

The June CPI print landed cleanly SOFT this morning at 8:30 AM ET, and the tape is rewarding the disinflation thesis. Headline CPI fell -0.4% MoM (energy did most of the work, with energy CPI collapsing -5.7% MoM), while the YoY rate slowed to +3.5% — the first annual deceleration since the Iran-war shock — versus consensus expectations of +3.8%. Core CPI printed flat at 0.0% MoM, with broad-based softness across motor vehicle insurance, communication services, apparel, medical care, and used vehicles, partially offset by modest gains in recreation, household furnishings, and personal care (BLS, July 14). The combination is Goldilocks: the inflation pulse that panicked markets into pre-CPI de-risking on Monday has now resolved dovishly, and the structural bull case is back on.

By the close of the cash session SPX was up +0.40% to 7,545.77 (from Mon's 7,515.34), the VIX was crushed -4.25% to 16.43, and the 10Y yield pulled back -2.4 bp to 4.585% (yfinance close). The 1-month forecast target of 7,500 has now been cleared — yesterday's positioning concern is resolved. The next resistance is the 7,610 area flagged in Monday's forecast; the 3-month target of 7,600 sits +0.72% above current, and the year-end target of 7,800 sits +3.37% above current.

Morning Commentary

The 8:30 AM ET print cleared the disinflation bar cleanly, and the tape rewarded it. Headline -0.4% MoM and YoY +3.5% (30 bp undershoot vs +3.8% consensus) is materially softer than the consensus base case had priced — particularly given the consensus expectation that energy would re-accelerate after the May Iran-war shock. Instead, energy CPI collapsed -5.7% MoM (gasoline -7.2%, utility gas -3.1%), and core CPI printed flat at 0.0% MoM with broad-based softness across motor vehicle insurance, communication, apparel, medical care, and used vehicles. The BLS press release calls out shelter as "meaningful relief in June" — which aligns with the market-base case that the shelter disinflation that began in Q1 2026 is now extending through Q2. This is unambiguously dovish relative to consensus expectations, and the bonds market responded first (10Y -2.4 bp to 4.585% within minutes of the print), then equities (SPX gapped higher into the open), then vol collapsed (VIX -4.25% to 16.43 — back below the 1-year mean of ~20).

The cross-asset tape confirmed the dovish reaction. Gold rallied +1.61% to $4,061 (a clean dovish-CPI signal — gold bid = real-rate compression thesis), the dollar deflated -0.36% to 100.92 (DXY), 10Y yields fell -2.4 bp (TNX), and 30Y yields held nearly flat (-0.08% at TYX 5.094% — note: the long end didn't rally as hard as the front end, suggesting the market is reading the print as "disinflation, but not recession"). The only cross-asset oddity is WTI crude at $79.58 (+1.84%) rising on the day — the Iran-Hormuz geopolitical premium is intact even as headline CPI energy collapsed on retail gasoline prices. This is a temporary cross-current, not a regime break: oil prices are wholesale (WTI), and the headline CPI is retail gasoline-reflected; the divergence should resolve as retail catches down within 2-3 weeks.

Sector rotation confirms the positioning unwind. Monday's pre-CPI de-risking pattern (XLK -2.49% / defensives bid / small caps lagging) fully reversed today: XLK +1.42% / QQQ +1.23% led the rally, defensives deflated (XLV -1.92%, XLP -1.39%) as the rate-cut conviction reactivated. Real estate (XLRE -0.48%) lagged despite lower yields because the sub-sector has been pricing duration compression for months — today's yield move wasn't enough to re-rate the entire REIT cohort higher in one session. Industrials (XLI) and consumer discretionary (XLY) were nearly flat — clean participation without overextension. Small caps (IWM +0.26%) participated modestly; the floating-rate-sensitivity drag wasn't a factor in a dovish-CPI session. The cleanest read: this is a relief rally that ALREADY priced Monday's positioning fear, NOT a fresh regime-shift higher.

The structural uptrend is now intact and the bear-case window has narrowed materially. Yesterday's PM forecast had the 1-month target at 7,500 with a binary risk that a hot CPI could push SPX back to retest the July 8 intraday low of 7,422. The 7,500 target has been cleared today (current 7,545.77 is -0.61% below it — actually, it's -0.61% ABOVE 7,500, so the target is met). The bear-case year-end 7,000 has narrowed because today's print reduces the probability of a Warsh-style hawkish SEP hike — the disinflation thesis holds and the base case is now sequencing rate cuts earlier than the prior consensus.

Three watchpoints for the rest of today and into Wednesday: (1) JPM and GS earnings after the close — NIM, provisions, capital return. The market is pricing a clean bank earnings cycle into the dovish-CPI backdrop; if JPM delivers NIM expansion, XLF pushes through $57 and the breadth of the rally extends. (2) Waller at 12:30 PM ET at NYABE — Waller has been a hawkish voice; any pushback against the dovish reaction would force a 10Y yield re-pricing and pull SPX off the highs. (3) PPI Wednesday at 8:30 AM ET — consensus +0.2% MoM / +2.8% YoY. A soft PPI confirmation closes the dovish-inflation-pair door (CPI+PPI both soft); a hot PPI forces the Fed to lean harder on the unemployment/labor side of the mandate and complicates the rate-cut sequencing. The PPI is the next material event on the calendar.

Bottom Line

The disinflation thesis held — yesterday's pre-CPI de-risking was positioning, not a thesis break. The 1-month target of 7,500 has been cleared with a soft CPI print, and the next tactical pivot is the 7,610 resistance. Bank earnings this evening and PPI Wednesday are the next binary tests; a clean combination (NIM expansion + soft PPI) would extend the rally toward the 3-month 7,600 target. The structural uptrend is intact and the bear-case window (year-end 7,000) has meaningfully narrowed.
