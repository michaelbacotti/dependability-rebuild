About Dependability Holdings LLC

Independent Market Research

Dependability Holdings LLC publishes independent financial research on S&P 500 market trends, volatility dynamics, and options strategy. Our work synthesizes Wall Street consensus, historical seasonality, and options market signals — without sell-side conflicts or promotional agendas. Every forecast ties its directional view to three explicit inputs, and every strategy guide publishes its risk parameters up front so readers can evaluate the reasoning themselves.

The site launched in March 2024 with a single weekday morning brief. It now publishes a regular morning analysis (7:00 AM ET, weekdays), an afternoon market report covering S&P 500 levels, volatility, and the next-week outlook, and a long-form archive of evergreen strategy guides — about 50 articles, 35 afternoon market reports, and a complete trade log covering every position we have placed in our own portfolio since launch.

Editorial Team

The site is edited by Marcus Hale, who oversees the editorial calendar, writes the long-form market commentary, and coordinates the research desk's coverage. Marcus has been writing about equities, volatility, and derivatives for fifteen years — first as a buy-side research analyst at two long/short hedge funds, then as the lead writer for an options education platform with about 40,000 newsletter subscribers, and now full-time on this site. He coordinates the three-person research desk and is responsible for the final editorial direction of every published forecast.

The desk includes a Quant Strategist who builds the volatility and seasonality models that feed the morning brief, and a Markets Reporter who covers breaking news and overnight catalysts. Each editor has a defined coverage area. Every forecast is reviewed by at least one other desk member before publication.

Our Research Framework

Every forecast starts with three inputs: (1) where Wall Street consensus sits on S&P 500 targets and earnings, (2) how current seasonality and historical market patterns compare to similar periods in prior cycles, and (3) what options market pricing implies about future volatility expectations — particularly in the VIX term structure and actual realized moves vs. implied moves.

These three pillars are weighted, tested against each other, and synthesized into a directional view with explicit risk parameters. We publish our methodology separately so readers can evaluate the reasoning behind every forecast. When the three inputs disagree — for example, when consensus is bullish but volatility pricing is nervous — we say so in the brief rather than forcing a synthetic consensus view.

Editorial Selection Process

About 60% of our coverage is driven by market events — earnings season, Federal Reserve meetings, CPI prints, jobs reports, geopolitical catalysts. The remaining 40% is editor-initiated: an evergreen piece on a specific strategy structure, a deep-dive on a particular volatility regime, or a synthesis of an emerging theme we have been tracking for several weeks. We do not write reviews or commentary based on tips, publicist pitches, or sample chapters. We do not publish pieces we have not fact-checked against primary sources.

Every article goes through a three-step review: (1) the writing editor drafts the piece, (2) a second desk member reads and challenges the reasoning, (3) Marcus approves the final copy. For published forecasts that turn out wrong, we publish a post-mortem within 30 days describing what we got right, what we got wrong, and how the model will be adjusted.

Options Strategy Research

Beyond market forecasting, we publish educational research on defined-risk options strategies — Iron Condors, Calendar Spreads, Bull Call Spreads, Covered Calls, and other structures that allow investors to express a market view with known, bounded risk. Every strategy guide covers entry criteria, position management, and exit rules.

We focus on strategies that survive the real world: wide-enough strikes to avoid premature assignment, position sizes calibrated to account for IV crush and gamma risk, and management rules that work across bull markets, bear markets, and range-bound periods alike.

Our Principles

  • Strategy guides emphasize risk management over profit optimization
  • No promotional content, no affiliate revenue from described strategies
  • All content is for informational purposes — not investment advice
