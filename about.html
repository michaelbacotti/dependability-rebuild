About Dependability Holdings LLC

Independent research. No conflicts. No investment products to sell.

Our Mission

Dependability Holdings LLC was founded on a single conviction: that rigorous, independent analysis — without the conflicts inherent in broker-dealer models — produces better research. We focus on options market dynamics, volatility regimes, and macro-driven positioning because that is where our expertise is deepest and where most retail and institutional investors face the most persistent information disadvantage.

Financial media is crowded with content that is either too simple to be useful or too complex to be actionable. The useful middle ground — analysis that takes the time to be right, explains its reasoning, and respects the reader's intelligence — is remarkably thin. We built Dependability to occupy that space: a research desk that publishes when it has something to say, explains what it actually thinks, and doesn't have a product to sell underneath every conclusion.

Why We Cover Options Markets

Options markets are where informed investors manage risk, express directional views with defined risk profiles, and extract premium from the volatility risk premium that exists in every market regime. They are also where the gap between professional and retail understanding is widest. Institutional participants pricing complex multi-leg structures, market makers hedging their exposure, institutional hedgers adjusting gamma — these dynamics move markets in ways that are difficult to observe without a structured framework. Our research tries to give informed readers that framework.

The VIX is a central focus because it is simultaneously the most misunderstood and most useful risk metric available to equity investors. Retail coverage tends to treat it as a fear gauge in simple terms — high VIX means scared, low VIX means complacent. Professional options analysis understands it as a implied volatility surface with a term structure, a skew, and a set of cross-market relationships that carry genuine predictive signal for subsequent returns. We write for the reader who wants to understand those dynamics, not just watch the number go up and down.

What We Cover

Our research covers the S&P 500 and its associated volatility surface (VIX, VIX futures, SPX options), with regular macro commentary on treasury yields, credit spreads, and economic data that moves markets. We publish weekly forecasts, market commentary 2–3 times per week, and educational content for traders who want to build genuine understanding rather than copy strategies they don't comprehend.

Within the options market, our focus spans the full chain: from front-month at-the-money implied volatility to the term structure of VIX futures, from standard put-call parity relationships to the practical realities of spread execution costs, from the mechanics of earnings-driven IV crush to the macro regimes that produce sustained volatility expansion. We believe that understanding the full chain produces better trading judgment than focusing on any single piece in isolation.

Our Approach

We take no compensation from any product we discuss. No market making, no promotional pitches, no affiliate arrangements. The research desk operates independently, and the only metric that matters is whether our analysis is honest, internally consistent, and useful to someone who is trying to make a well-informed decision. We will tell you when we are uncertain. We will update our views as data changes. We will not hold positions in the securities we write about.

Our analytical process begins with primary data — CBOE market statistics, OCC position data, FRED economic releases, SEC filings — and builds outward through structured interpretation. We do not use technical analysis patterns or momentum signals as primary inputs; our framework is built on macro regime identification, volatility surface analysis, and options-specific metrics (term structure, skew, put-call ratios, gamma exposure) combined with fundamental economic data. Every forecast includes an explicit confidence level and a description of the key assumptions that would need to change for us to revise our view.

Our Methodology

Our methodology is documented in full on our Methodology page. The short version: we use a combination of quantitative screens (VIX term structure shape, skew metrics, put-call volume ratios) and qualitative macro judgment to identify regime changes in the volatility market. We do not publish trade alerts. We do not maintain a live portfolio. We publish research that reflects our current best thinking, with the goal of helping sophisticated investors form their own views more clearly.

We update our major forecasts when the data changes materially — not on a fixed schedule. A forecast that remains valid does not get a "refresh" just to appear current. If our last published view on the VIX term structure is still our view, we will say so in our commentary rather than manufacturing a new headline. We believe this produces more useful research than the content-mill approach of appearing to have new thoughts on a fixed cadence.
