Calendar Spread Strike Selection: ATM for Neutral, OTM for Directional Lean

June 04, 2026  |  Dependability Research Desk

The strike selection for a calendar spread determines the position's directional exposure, vega, and breakeven range. An at-the-money (ATM) calendar spread is a neutral trade that profits from the IV crush without a strong directional view. A slightly out-of-the-money (OTM) calendar spread is a directional trade that adds a bullish or bearish bias to the IV crush. The choice between them depends on the trader's view on the underlying and the IV environment.

This article covers the strike selection options, the directional implications, the breakeven range, and the practical decision rule for selecting the strike.

The Three Strike Categories

For a calendar spread, the strike of both legs (the long and short) is the same. The choice is which strike to use:

Strike Category Position vs Current Price Directional Bias Vega
At the money (ATM) At the current price Neutral Highest
Slightly OTM (1-3%) Above (for calls) or below (for puts) the current price Slight directional lean High
Deep OTM (5%+) Well above (for calls) or well below (for puts) Strong directional bias Low

The default for an IV crush trade is ATM. The strike should be adjusted to OTM only when the trader has a specific directional view.

The ATM Calendar Spread

An ATM calendar spread has both legs at the current underlying price. The position is delta-neutral at entry (delta of the near-term leg roughly offsets the delta of the longer-dated leg, both of which are near zero for ATM options).

Parameter ATM Calendar
Delta at entry Near zero (delta-neutral)
Vega Highest (most sensitive to IV changes)
Breakeven range Symmetric around the strike
Profit zone Widest in percentage terms
Directional risk Lowest

The ATM calendar is the purest IV crush trade. The position profits from the differential between the near-term and longer-dated IV declines, and the directional risk is minimal.

Example: Stock at $150, ATM calendar spread (sell $150 call 14 DTE, buy $150 call 45 DTE).

  • Stock stays at $150: near-term expires worthless, longer-dated retains time value → maximum profit
  • Stock moves to $155: near-term has $5 intrinsic, longer-dated has $5+ intrinsic but more time value → small profit or breakeven
  • Stock moves to $160: near-term has $10 intrinsic, longer-dated has $10 intrinsic but less time value → loss

The ATM calendar is profitable as long as the stock stays within roughly $5 of the strike at expiration of the near-term option. Beyond that range, the position is at a loss.

The Slightly OTM Calendar Spread

A slightly OTM calendar spread has both legs 1-3% above (for calls) or below (for puts) the current underlying price. The position is delta-positive (for calls) or delta-negative (for puts), which adds a directional bias to the IV crush.

Parameter Slightly OTM Calendar
Delta at entry Positive (for calls) or negative (for puts)
Vega High but lower than ATM
Breakeven range Shifted in the direction of the bias
Profit zone Narrower than ATM but shifted
Directional risk Moderate

The OTM calendar is appropriate when the trader has a directional view and wants to combine the IV crush with a bullish or bearish position.

Example: Stock at $150, slightly OTM call calendar (sell $152 call 14 DTE, buy $152 call 45 DTE).

  • Stock stays at $150: near-term expires worthless, longer-dated has small time value → smaller profit than ATM
  • Stock moves to $152: near-term expires worthless, longer-dated has $0 intrinsic + time value → moderate profit
  • Stock moves to $155: near-term has $3 intrinsic, longer-dated has $3 intrinsic + time value → moderate profit
  • Stock moves to $160: near-term has $8 intrinsic, longer-dated has $8 intrinsic but less time value → loss

The OTM calendar is profitable as long as the stock moves toward the strike (in the direction of the bias). The position has a directional sweet spot around the strike.

The Deep OTM Calendar Spread

A deep OTM calendar spread has both legs 5%+ above (for calls) or below (for puts) the current underlying price. The position is strongly delta-positive (or negative) but has low vega. The position is essentially a directional bet with a small IV crush component.

Parameter Deep OTM Calendar
Delta at entry High (positive for calls)
Vega Low (limited IV sensitivity)
Breakeven range Far from the current price
Profit zone Narrow, requires a large move
Directional risk High

The deep OTM calendar is rarely used. The low vega means the IV crush component is too small to be meaningful, and the high delta means the position is essentially a long call (or long put) with extra cost. A naked long call would be more cost-efficient.

The IV Crush Size by Strike

The IV crush is not the same for all strikes. ATM options have the largest absolute IV change because their pre-event IV is highest. OTM options have smaller IV changes because their pre-event IV is lower.

Strike Pre-Event IV Post-Event IV IV Change
ATM 90% 40% -50 pts
2% OTM (calls) 75% 35% -40 pts
5% OTM (calls) 55% 30% -25 pts
10% OTM (calls) 35% 25% -10 pts

The ATM strike has the largest IV crush. The OTM strikes have progressively smaller IV crushes, and the deep OTM strikes have almost no IV crush.

For an IV crush trade, the ATM strike is the most efficient because the IV crush is largest. For a directional trade, the OTM strike is appropriate because the position has a directional bias.

The Directional Trade-Off

The choice between ATM and OTM is a trade-off between the IV crush capture and the directional exposure.

Strike IV Crush Capture Directional Exposure Best For
ATM Maximum None Pure IV crush trade
Slightly OTM (1-3%) Large Moderate IV crush with directional bias
Deep OTM (5%+) Small High Directional bet (use naked call instead)

The default for an earnings trade is ATM. The trader who has a specific directional view (e.g., "I think the stock will move up after earnings") may shift to slightly OTM to add the directional bias.

The Breakeven Implications

The breakeven range of a calendar spread depends on the strike selection. The ATM calendar has a symmetric breakeven around the strike; the OTM calendar has an asymmetric breakeven.

Strike Breakeven Range (10% IV Crush) Profit Zone Width
ATM $145 to $155 (10% of $150) $10
2% OTM (call, $152 strike) $148 to $158 $10 (shifted up)
5% OTM (call, $158 strike) $152 to $168 $16 (shifted up, but smaller IV crush)

The OTM calendar has a wider profit zone in absolute terms (because the strike is higher) but a smaller IV crush. The ATM calendar has a smaller profit zone in absolute terms but a larger IV crush. The choice depends on the trader's view.

The Decision Rule

A practical algorithm for strike selection:

  1. No directional view: Use ATM. The position is a pure IV crush trade, and the breakeven range is symmetric.
  2. Slight bullish view (stock expected to drift up): Use 1-2% OTM call calendar. The position adds a bullish bias to the IV crush.
  3. Slight bearish view (stock expected to drift down): Use 1-2% OTM put calendar. The position adds a bearish bias to the IV crush.
  4. Strong directional view: Skip the calendar spread. Use a debit spread or a naked call/put. The calendar spread's capped directional exposure does not match a strong directional view.
  5. Earnings trade (default): Use ATM. The IV crush is largest at the strike, and the position is directionally neutral.

The decision is view-driven. The trader who has no view uses ATM; the trader who has a view shifts to OTM in the direction of the view.

The Striking Distance Rule

A useful rule: the strike should be within 1.0 standard deviation of the current underlying price at the time of the event. The expected move of the stock over the holding period is the standard deviation; the strike should be within that range.

For a stock at $150 with 20% annualized volatility and a 14-day event:

  • Daily volatility: 20% / sqrt(252) = 1.26%
  • 14-day volatility: 1.26% × sqrt(14) = 4.7%
  • 1 standard deviation: $150 × 4.7% = $7

The strike should be within $7 of $150, which means strikes from $143 to $157 are within 1 standard deviation. The ATM strike ($150) is the center of the range.

The Volatility Smile

The volatility smile (or skew) affects the strike selection. For most equities, the put skew is steeper than the call skew, which means OTM puts have higher IV than OTM calls at the same distance from the money. The implication:

  • For a put calendar (bearish bias), the IV crush is larger for OTM strikes than for ATM strikes.
  • For a call calendar (bullish bias), the IV crush is smaller for OTM strikes than for ATM strikes.

A put calendar at the same percentage OTM as a call calendar will have a larger IV crush. The trader who is bearish should consider a put calendar; the trader who is bullish should consider a call calendar but expect a smaller IV crush per percentage OTM.

The Earnings Volatility Pattern

The pre-earnings volatility is not flat across strikes. The at-the-money IV is highest, and the OTM IV is lower. After earnings, the IV crush is largest at the ATM strike and smaller at the OTM strikes. The result is that the ATM strike is the most efficient for capturing the IV crush.

Strike Pre-Earnings IV Post-Earnings IV IV Crush Dollar Impact (per spread)
ATM ($150) 90% 40% -50 pts $3.00 (50 × 0.06 vega)
2% OTM ($153) 78% 38% -40 pts $2.40 (40 × 0.06 vega)
5% OTM ($158) 58% 33% -25 pts $1.50 (25 × 0.06 vega)

The ATM strike captures the largest dollar amount of the IV crush. The OTM strikes capture less. The trade-off is the directional exposure: the OTM strikes have a directional bias that the ATM strike does not.
