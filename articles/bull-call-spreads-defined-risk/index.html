Bull Call Spreads: Defined-Risk Directional Exposure

May 20, 2026  |  Dependability Research Desk

When you expect a moderate move higher — enough to be profitable but not a runaway move — a bull call spread offers defined-risk directional exposure at a fraction of the cost of buying naked calls. Buying a naked call is expensive in elevated IV environments; the spread reduces cost while maintaining upside participation.

Structure

Buy a call at a lower strike; sell a call at a higher strike. Same expiration. Net debit. The long call captures the upside; the short call funds it. Max profit = the difference in strikes minus the net debit paid.

Key Metrics

  • Strike selection: Buy 2–3 strikes below the expected move
  • Duration: Target 30–45 DTE
  • Cost: Net debit should be no more than 50% of the intrinsic value of the long call at entry
  • Position sizing: Risk no more than 1–2% of capital on a single spread

Exit Rules

Take profit when the long call reaches 2x the debit paid. Move the short strike up (rolling) if the underlying breaks through your target. Don't hold past 2 weeks before expiration if underperforming.
