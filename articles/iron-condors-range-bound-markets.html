Iron Condors: Premium Collection in Range-Bound Markets

May 15, 2026  |  Dependability Research Desk

When the market is trading in a well-defined range with no clear directional catalyst, the iron condor is among the most effective tools available to an options trader. The strategy collects premium from time decay while maintaining a wide enough range to tolerate modest volatility without taking losses.

Structure

Sell an OTM put spread at lower strikes + sell an OTM call spread at upper strikes. Both spreads are outside the expected trading range. The wider the wings, the more profit potential but also more risk if the underlying breaks out.

Key Metrics

  • Duration: Target 30–45 days to expiration
  • Wing width: 15–20 S&P points (or equivalent percentage for other underlyings)
  • Credit received: $1.50–$2.50 per spread
  • Max risk: Strike width minus credit received

Exit Rules

Take profit at 50% of max profit. Stop out if the short strike is breached and the long leg cannot be rolled. Never hold through a major event — FOMC, CPI, earnings — without adjusting.
