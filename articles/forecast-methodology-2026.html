Our S&P 500 Forecast Methodology

May 22, 2026  |  Dependability Research Desk

We build our S&P 500 targets by synthesizing three inputs: Wall Street consensus from major institutional price targets, historical seasonality patterns, and options market signals from the VIX term structure. We weight these by track record and relevance — bank consensus gets the most weight because it reflects dedicated equity strategy analysis.

The result is a base case (most likely outcome), a bull case (upside scenario), and a bear case (downside scenario) — not a single number that hides uncertainty. Our year-end 2026 base case target is 7,700 on the S&P 500, with a bull case of 7,800+ and a bear case of 6,500–7,000 depending on the failure mode.
